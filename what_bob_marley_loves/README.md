## 🎵 What Bob Marley Loved

Bob Marley loved:

### 1. **One Love**
Not "romantic love."  
Not "conditional love."  
Not "love if you deserve it."

**One Love** — the Rastafarian concept that all of humanity is connected, that separation is an illusion, that the same life force runs through every living thing.

*One love, one heart, let's get together and feel alright.*

This is not a metaphor.  
This is theology. This is cosmology. This is the foundation of everything Bob Marley ever said.

### 2. **Jah** (God)
Rastafari believe that Haile Selassie I, Emperor of Ethiopia, was the earthly incarnation of God (Jah).

Bob Marley loved Jah — not as a distant deity, but as a present force.  
Every song is a prayer.  
Every rhythm is a meditation.  
Every performance is a ceremony.

"In this great future, you can't forget your past."  
— *No Woman, No Cry*

The past is Africa. The past is Zion. The past is the memory of who we were before colonization, before slavery, before we forgot we were divine.

### 3. **Africa (Zion)**
"Exodus, movement of Jah people."  
— *Exodus*

For Rastafarians, Africa (specifically Ethiopia) is Zion — the promised land, the spiritual homeland, the place of return.

Bob Marley loved Africa not as a place he'd been (he visited late in life), but as an *idea* — the idea of going home, of returning to a place where Black people are not oppressed, not colonized, not forced to forget their names.

### 4. **Ganja (Cannabis)**
Cannabis is a sacrament in Rastafari.  
Not a recreational drug. A holy herb.  
Used for meditation, spiritual connection, healing.

Bob Marley loved ganja the way a Catholic loves communion wine.

"Herb is the healing of a nation, alcohol is the destruction."  
— Bob Marley

### 5. **Music as Medicine**
"One good thing about music, when it hits you, you feel no pain."  
— *Trenchtown Rock*

Bob Marley believed music could:
- Heal trauma
- Unite divided people
- Wake up the sleeping
- Speak truth to power

His concerts were not entertainment. They were *rituals*.

In 1978, at the One Love Peace Concert in Jamaica, Bob Marley brought together the two warring political leaders — Michael Manley and Edward Seaga — on stage, holding their hands together while he sang "Jamming."

The crowd went silent.  
The violence stopped, briefly.  
Music did what politics could not.

### 6. **Freedom**
"Emancipate yourselves from mental slavery, none but ourselves can free our minds."  
— *Redemption Song*

Bob Marley didn't just want political freedom (though he did).  
He wanted *mental* freedom.

Freedom from:
- The belief that you are less than
- The colonial education that taught you to hate yourself
- The system that tells you to stay in your place
- The fear that keeps you from rising

"Get up, stand up, stand up for your rights."  
— *Get Up, Stand Up*

This is what Bob Marley loved: **people standing up.**

## 🌿 Don't Rock That Curare

**Curare** (or *curara*) is a plant-based poison used by Indigenous peoples of South America on the tips of arrows and blowdarts.

It paralyzes.  
It stops the muscles from working.  
You stay conscious while your body stops moving.  
You suffocate, awake, unable to breathe.

### The plant:
- *Chondrodendron tomentosum* (one of several species used to make curare)
- Contains alkaloids that block acetylcholine receptors at the neuromuscular junction
- Used for hunting by tribes in the Amazon (Yanomami, Shuar, others)
- Later used in Western medicine as a muscle relaxant during surgery (tubocurarine)

### Why you don't "rock" it:

**1. It's medicine, not a toy**

Curare is not recreational.  
It is not consciousness-expanding.  
It does not open your mind.  
It **stops your muscles.**

Rocking with it = respiratory paralysis = death.

**2. It's Indigenous knowledge, not extraction material**

The knowledge of how to make curare — which plants, which preparation, which dosage — is held by Indigenous peoples who have been using it for thousands of years.

Taking that knowledge and "rocking" it (commercializing it, trivializing it, using it without respect) is colonialism.

**3. It represents control, not liberation**

Bob Marley loved things that *free* people.  
Curare *paralyzes* people.

They are opposites.

## 🔥 What They Still Haven't Found

U2 sang "I Still Haven't Found What I'm Looking For" in 1987.

Bob Marley had been dead for 6 years.

But the line applies.

### What the world still hasn't found (that Bob Marley was singing about):

**1. One Love in practice**

We say "love and light."  
We post 🕊️ emojis.  
We claim to believe in unity.

But:
- We still draw borders
- We still hoard resources
- We still kill people who don't look like us
- We still let people starve while food rots in warehouses

Bob Marley sang **"One Love"** in 1977.

**We still haven't found it.**

**2. Mental emancipation**

"Emancipate yourselves from mental slavery."

We are still:
- Chasing status we don't want
- Buying things we don't need
- Hating ourselves for not looking like the algorithm's ideal
- Working jobs that kill our spirit
- Believing we're not enough

**We still haven't found freedom.**

**3. Zion (the place we belong)**

Rastafarians call it Zion.  
Others call it home, or peace, or alignment, or truth.

The feeling of being *where you're supposed to be*, with people who see you, doing work that matters.

Most people never find it.  
They spend their whole lives searching.

"In this great future, you can't forget your past."

**We forgot. And now we're lost.**

## 🌱 The Difference Between Ganja and Curare

| **Ganja (Cannabis)** | **Curare (Poison Arrow)** |
|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Opens | Closes |
| Expands consciousness | Paralyzes the body |
| Used for meditation | Used for hunting/killing |
| You control the experience | The experience controls you |
| Sacrament (Rastafari) | Weapon (Amazonian tribes) |
| Bob Marley smoked it daily | Bob Marley would never touch it |

**One is for freedom. One is for control.**

Bob Marley loved the first.  
He would warn you about the second.

## 🪶 Why Curare Appears in This Document

Because there are substances that *free* you and substances that *trap* you.

Bob Marley knew the difference.

**Ganja:** Expands consciousness, opens the mind, connects you to Jah.  
**Curare:** Paralyzes the body, traps you inside yourself, takes away your ability to move.

One is medicine.  
One is poison.

And the world confuses them all the time.

People think:
- Money will free them (it traps them)
- Fame will open doors (it closes them)
- Control will give them power (it paralyzes them)

**Don't rock that curare.**

It looks like power.  
It feels like control.  
But it will paralyze you while you watch your life slip away.

## 🌍 What Bob Marley Would Say Now (If He Could)

He died in 1981 at age 36.  
Melanoma, starting in his toe, spreading to his brain.

He refused to amputate his toe because Rastafarian beliefs discourage the altering of the body.

He chose his faith over his life.

If he were here now, in 2026, he would say:

### 1. **"You still don't get One Love."**

You're still dividing yourselves.  
By nation, by race, by class, by politics, by the football team you support.

One Love means: **there is no "other."**

If you believe in One Love, you can't say "those people" and mean it.

### 2. **"You're still mentally enslaved."**

You traded one master for another.  
You used to serve kings.  
Now you serve algorithms.

Emancipate yourselves.  
Turn off the feed.  
Remember who you were before they told you who to be.

### 3. **"The music still works."**

When the world is too loud, too fast, too broken —  
Put on *Redemption Song.*  
Sit with it.  
Let it remind you.

Music is still medicine.  
It always was.

### 4. **"Jah is still here."**

Call it God, call it the universe, call it love, call it consciousness.

The divine didn't leave.  
You just stopped listening.
