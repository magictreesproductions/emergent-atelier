**Miraculin** is a glycoprotein found in the *miracle berry*  
(*Synsepalum dulcificum*) from West Africa.

Then you eat a lemon.

The acid activates the miraculin molecules.  
They fold into a new shape.  
They trigger every sweet receptor you have.  
All at once.  

And the lemon —

**tastes like the sweetest lemonade you have ever drunk.**

Nothing changed in the lemon.

## 🌊 THE SCIENCE MEETS THE SOUL

### Synsepalum dulcificum
- **Origin:** West Africa (Ghana, Nigeria)
- **Traditional use:** Eaten before sour local foods to make them palatable
- **Western discovery:** 1725, by explorer Chevalier des Marchais
- **Modern use:** Flavor-tripping parties, cancer patient taste therapy, sugar-free cooking

### Why It Matters Beyond the Tongue

The medical applications are profound:  
Chemotherapy destroys taste receptors.  
Cancer patients often cannot eat because everything tastes  
metallic, bitter, wrong.  
Miraculin can restore sweetness to food.
